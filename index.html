Manifesto

What Is SOUP?

A Commons, Not a Feed

SOUP is built on the idea that knowledge should be archived, not buried. Every video, blog post, and file lives in a permanent, searchable channel. No algorithms deciding what you see. No infinite scroll designed to addict. Just intentional content, organized by people who care.

Channels, Not Profiles

A person is not a brand. On SOUP, one account runs many channels. "Austin Farming," "Dev Logs," "Medical Notes," "Detroit Makers" — each is its own space with its own contributors and rules. Separate identity from topic. Let ideas stand alone.

Files Are First-Class

Most platforms treat file sharing as an afterthought. SOUP has a dedicated File Lane — PDFs, datasets, templates, tools. Every file carries a SHA-256 integrity hash and a license tag. Download, verify, trust. Distribution is the point, not the side effect.

You Own the Kitchen

SOUP is self-hosted. Your data lives on your server, not in a Silicon Valley warehouse. One PostgreSQL database, one Nginx reverse proxy, one kitchen — serving many dishes. No terms-of-service changes that lock you out of your own work.

Trust Model

Trust Is Everything

A file-sharing platform lives or dies on integrity. These are non-negotiable.

Integrity Hashes

Every file displays its SHA-256 checksum. Download, verify, trust. No tampered PDFs or infected executables slipping through.

sha256: a3f2..8e1d

Quarantine Scan

Unknown files sit in quarantine until scanned. No auto-execution. No silent downloads. Manual approval for new uploaders.

No Private Messages

Public channels only. No DM vector for harassment or phishing. If you need to talk, fork a post and reply in the open.

No Algorithm

Chronological order. Channel subscriptions. Search by tag and license. What you follow is what you see. Full stop.
